About Me

I'm a junior undergraduate at South China University of Technology (SCUT), majoring in Big Data Management and Application. My research sits at the intersection of healthcare and interpretable AI — I believe medical AI systems must not only be accurate but also explainable to clinicians and patients.

My journey started at the AI Research Institute of Guangdong No. 2 Provincial People's Hospital, where I worked on LLM fine-tuning and knowledge graph construction for cardiovascular diseases. Since then, I've built multiple systems that combine large language models with structured medical knowledge — from a standardized patient Q&A system processing 8,000 clinical cases to a myocardial ischemia knowledge graph with XGBoost-based reasoning.

I'm also passionate about building AI tools that feel human. Viora, my AI health companion, proactively checks in on users through natural conversation rather than forms and checklists. Portraiture explores whether we can build digital twins from chat logs that are evidence-backed and interpretable rather than black-box.
